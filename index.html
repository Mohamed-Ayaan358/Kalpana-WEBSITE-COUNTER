What is KALPANA?

"Nothing is impossible for women if they have a strong will". These were the words sent by Kalpana Chawla to all Indians, especially young girls. The name of this event is in remembrance of this woman who taught girls to dream of wings. She was the first woman of Indian origin to travel into space on NASAâ€™s Space Shuttle mission, Columbia. Although we lost her in a tragic Space Shuttle disaster, she remains an epitome of inspiration and motivation for all the men and women around the world and especially for the aspiring youth of India. So this event is an opportunity for all the intellect heads out there seeking for opportunities to prove that they got that will inside them.

The Women In Engineering [WiE] Chapter at IEEE PESIT South Student Branch presents to you an event that aims to bring Technology and Humanitarianism under one roof. This event consists of two main facets:-

Technology:
This facet of Kalpana welcomes all engineering students from colleges across Karnataka to compete and showcase their technical skills in the Battle of Innovations to solve real-world problems that exist in our society today. A stringent selection process will shortlist the teams who provide the best solutions for the problem statements provided on the day of the event in subsequent rounds of judging. These teams will compete with each other to be the ultimate winner. Depending on the solution provided by the team, they will be given a part to improvise in their product as a challenge. This will be provided on the final stage of the event.

Humanitarianism :
With "Wear a mask and wash your hands" on repeat atleast a dozen times a day , we wonder why there's so much attention given to physical health and so little to mental health!

KALPANA 2020 presents to you, a talk to create awareness and normalise conversations around mental health in association with Ekam. We're immensely pleasured to announce that the founder of Ekam and a famous personality, Ms. PREETHA JI would be the speaker of our event. She's an eminent practitioner of mental and emotional well-being, she had given various TED Talks, and is the founder of "One World Academy", a philosophy and meditation school. Join us and LET'S TALK about MENTAL HEALTH!

We at PESIT South Student Branch trust that we have a moral responsibility as future engineers to give back to the community we live in. We hope that you believe in the same spirit and participate in Kalpana 2020.

Procedure:

Step 1:

Participants register in teams of 2-3 for the CTF. Teams of 4 (or more) and individuals are also welcome to participate, but will not be considered as candidates for the prize pool. Individuals who could not find teammates can be assigned teams with other individual participants if they consent, on a first come first serve basis.

Step 2:

The competition will begin on 6th November, at 6 p.m. All participants will be added to a discord server two days prior to the event, so they can familiarise themselves with the server, and individuals can be assigned teams (if necessary) too. Support staff will be made available to assist challengers and clarify their questions, if need be. Links will be provided to the questions belonging to different categories.

The different categories of problems are:
  • General Flag Detection
  • Binary Exploitation
  • Cryptography
  • Forensics
  • Web Exploitation
  • Machine Cracking


Step 3:

The competition will last 48 hours, and will come to a close at 6:00 p.m, Nov 8th. The top three teams will be decided using the leaderboard, and will be announced the following day, bringing us to the business end of the competition.

All the best!

Rules

CTF

  1. Participants are allowed to refer to and use any resources they want in trying to solve the problems.
  2. Tie-breaking will be done only in case there is a tie for one of the top 3 places. In case of such a tie, the tie will be broken in the following ways.

    • The team which managed to reach the tied number of points first will be considered to have a higher rank
    • If multiple teams managed to reach the said number of points within 2minutes of the first team that reached those many points, then the difficulty of the problems that were solved will be taken into consideration
    • If the difficulty of the problems that were solved is the same, then the approach adopted in solving the hardest problems that each team solved would be taken into consideration.

  3. While participants are allowed to refer to any resource they find fit online, discussion between teams regarding solutions will strictly not be encouraged.
  4. If suspected of malpractice, teams that have solved the machines may also have to provide a write-up (upon request) explaining their approach and thought process.
  5. All participants are required to have a discord account. The discord username will be required to be provided during registration as well. In case a participant changes their discord username sometime between the close of registrations, and start of the event, they are required to notify the CTF Team of the same.

Rules

IOTify

Form

CTF

CTF (Capture The Flag) is an Information Security competition that challenges contestants to solve a variety of tasks ranging from basic text ciphers and inspecting code of a website to solving complex programming challenges and owning root access of virtual machines.

Participants will be given access to these challenges via the CTFd platform and submit the flags to the same platform, earn points, and try to claim the top spot on the live leaderboard.

Date: 6th to 8th November

Team size: 1-3 recommended. Participants can take part in teams of 4 too, but wouldn't be considered. If they don't have any team members, we can match individual registrations up too.

IOTIFY

IOTify is an event based on the Internet of things where the participant has to solve a given problem statement by designing a model and coding its working using Tinkercad, an IoT simulator. Select your domain during the registration.

Participants will select their desired domain during the registration procedure and based on their selection a problem statement will be assigned to each team on the day of the event. Teams are required to solve this problem statement and present their ideas and model to the judges. These models will go through three rounds of grading process before the winner is announced.

Date of event : 4th and 5th of November

Team size: 1 to 3 per team.
